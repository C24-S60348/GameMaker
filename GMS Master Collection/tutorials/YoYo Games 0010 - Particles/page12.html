Final Notes

As you can see, particles are an incredibly powerful and effective way to create cheap, yet impressive effects for your games. However, actually designing them can be a chore, especially when you have to test and test again to get an effect just right. thankfully a number of particle editors have been created by users of GameMaker:Studio and most of these can be export text files that contain all the necessary code which you just need to copy and paste into a script or your object. Two of them are:

Particle Designer 2.5 by Alert games
Sparks by Nocturne Games

but you can search around the internet for more as there are quite a few now.

You should also be aware that particles in HTML5 are not quite as fast and if WebGL is not switched on then they will not have color blending either, and that on Android/iOS devices, particles can slow down the games if they involve too much overdraw of the screen.

That's it for this tutorial and we hope you have fun with particles in your games!
